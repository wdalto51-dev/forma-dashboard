"Forward head posture directly alters craniofacial morphology by changing the resting position of the mandible and the loading pattern of the temporomandibular joint."
Journal of Oral Rehabilitation, 2010. Used clinically to explain malocclusion development in adolescents with poor spinal posture. The cervical spine governs how the skull sits — which governs how the mandible rests at all times.

02 · High Performance
Your desk setup is
restricting your oxygen.

Forward head posture compresses thoracic volume. Less thoracic volume means less forced vital capacity — FVC. Less FVC means your brain is operating on restricted oxygen. The prefrontal cortex is the first to degrade under mild hypoxia. This is not theory. This is the physiological mechanism behind the afternoon brain fog you've normalised.

The FVC / Oxygen Mechanism — Respiratory Physiology
"Thoracic kyphosis and forward head posture reduce FVC and FEV1 by measurably compressing thoracic volume. At 30% reduced lung capacity, cognitive performance declines are measurable within hours of sustained poor posture."
Multiple respiratory physiology studies confirm this pathway. Mechanism: restricted FVC → increased CO2 → cerebral vasodilation → reduced prefrontal cortex activation. This is the 3pm crash. It's mechanical, not metabolic. And it's fixable.
Fatigue Reduction in Knowledge Workers — ERQ 2019
"Correcting spinal alignment in knowledge workers reduced self-reported fatigue by 43% and increased afternoon productivity scores significantly over a 6-week intervention period."
Ergonomics Research Quarterly, 2019. Not a supplement. Not a productivity app. A mechanical correction to the variable that governs your oxygen supply. 43% fatigue reduction in 6 weeks.
The Sympathetic Nervous System Pathway — Manual Therapies Literature
"C1–C4 cervical mechanoreceptors feed directly into the sympathetic chain. Misalignment maintains a low-grade fight-or-flight state with a hormonal profile indistinguishable from chronic low-level stress — elevated cortisol, reduced HRV, suppressed parasympathetic tone."
Chiropractic & Manual Therapies literature. You can meditate, breathe, take adaptogens — and still run a sympathetic activation 8 hours a day from your desk posture. Every stress management protocol has limited efficacy if the mechanical input is still present.
